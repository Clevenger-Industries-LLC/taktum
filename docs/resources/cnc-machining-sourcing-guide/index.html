Define the machining package

Machining RFQs work best when 3D files, 2D drawings, tolerances, materials, finishes, quantities, and inspection requirements are all available before suppliers quote.

  • 3D files and drawings
  • Tolerance callouts
  • Inspection requirements

Avoid mismatched suppliers

A job that needs turning, 5-axis milling, production volume, or tight inspection may require very different shops. Supplier qualification should reflect the exact process and risk profile.

  • Turning
  • Milling
  • Production and inspection fit

Keep quote and production context together

Taktum keeps the original request, quote responses, production updates, quality notes, and delivery records connected so buyers and shops can see the same work history.

  • Quote responses
  • Quality notes
  • Delivery records

Match machining work to the right equipment profile

A turned part, plate-milled component, tight-tolerance prototype, and production-volume run can require different machine capacity and inspection discipline. Machining sourcing should capture equipment needs, tolerance risk, material behavior, and setup complexity before routing.

  • Turning or milling
  • Tolerance risk
  • Setup complexity

Preserve revision and inspection context

Machined parts often change as drawings mature or inspection findings come back. Taktum keeps revision context, supplier assumptions, quality notes, and delivery records connected to the same project so future orders can start with better information.

  • Revision history
  • Inspection notes
  • Repeat-order context

Machining sourcing depends on inspection and repeatability

A machining supplier can quote a prototype and still be the wrong fit for repeat production, tight inspection, or short-turn delivery. Buyers need to preserve tolerance risk, inspection plans, setup assumptions, material notes, and prior delivery outcomes. Taktum gives machining RFQs an operating record so future orders can reuse proven fit instead of rebuilding supplier confidence from scratch.

  • Tolerance risk
  • Inspection planning
  • Repeat-order confidence

FAQ

What makes a CNC machining RFQ complete?

A complete CNC machining RFQ includes CAD files, drawings, materials, tolerances, quantities, finishes, inspection requirements, delivery needs, and any special handling notes.

How does Taktum route machining work?

Taktum uses structured job data, manufacturer capability profiles, availability, and performance signals to route machining work toward qualified shops.

What files should a machining buyer provide?

A machining buyer should provide 3D CAD files, 2D drawings with tolerances, material requirements, finish notes, quantities, inspection expectations, deadlines, and any critical feature guidance.

How does Taktum help compare machine shops?

Taktum helps compare machine shops by keeping quote responses, capability fit, availability, quality notes, production updates, and supplier performance signals attached to the same request.
